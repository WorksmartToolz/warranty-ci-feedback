§ 01

Signal Summary for Quality Leadership

Read Time · 60 sec
⟶ The One Thing

One product line, one component family, is driving the warranty book.

TerraTrak-2P accounts for 55% of 2025 spend ($4.17M) and 78% of 2026 YTD spend ($324K). Within it, four components — Row Box, Slew Gear, Motor, and A-Frame — make up the active-claim backlog. This is a concentrated, actionable signal: a CI program that moves the needle on these four components will move the needle on the whole warranty book.

Meanwhile, Sunflower's 120-claim volume in 2025 points to a different CI problem — high-frequency/low-severity, likely a parts-quality or process-repeatability issue, not a design issue. And the 21 claim denials in 2026 are almost entirely process/documentation failures — an internal CI opportunity worth its own workstream.

§ 08

Recommended CI Program — Ranked by Leverage

Next 90 Days

  1. Stand up a TT-2P Component CAPA Program — Row Box, Slew Gear, Motor, A-Frame

    One program, four workstreams. These four components represent essentially the entire 2026 TT-2P active backlog and drove the majority of 2025 spend. Run DFMEA refresh on Row Box (cross-platform), supplier quality reviews on Slew Gear and Motor, and a load/installation review on A-Frame.

    Expect this to take 2–3 quarters to produce design/ECN output, but interim site-visit-to-parts-only conversions can deliver savings in the current fiscal year.

    Lead VP Eng + SQE
    High Impact

  2. Fix the Claims Intake Process Before Engineering Fixes the Products

    18 of 21 denials in 2026 YTD are process failures (duplicates, insufficient evidence, wrong info). Before engineering spends a dollar on redesign, Quality should spend a week updating the claims intake SOP — field validation on claim submission, mandatory evidence fields, duplicate detection at entry.

    Low cost, fast payback, and it produces cleaner data for every downstream CI analysis on this page.

    Lead Quality Mgr
    Med Impact · Fast

  3. Launch a Sunflower TCU Pattern Investigation

    Sunflower is a different beast from TT-2P — high volume (120 claims in 2025), lower per-unit severity, TCU RMA concentrated. This is likely a firmware, comms, or assembly-variance issue. Run claim-description text clustering on 2025 Sunflower data, cross-reference firmware rev-level and ECN history at time of install.

    Lead Sunflower PE
    Med Impact

  4. Close the $33.7M Documentation Gap

    Not a failure signal — a records signal. 38 portfolio projects have no warranty dates on file; TerraTrak alone is $29.3M of that. This is not about coverage — coverage likely exists in POs and sales agreements. It's about being able to defend the position in an audit, claim dispute, or insurance review.

    A records-recovery sprint (locate original PO, enter dates, confirm terms) is a no-engineering-cost way to eliminate a very real audit-risk exposure.

    Lead Contract Admin
    Med Impact · Low Cost

  5. Instrument Better Data — Claim-Level Granularity

    The Quality team is trying to do CI on pre-aggregated summary data. There is no row-level claim log, no monthly trend, no per-claim contract-type mapping, no MWdc exposure normalization. Every future CI decision would be sharper with those fields.

    Partner with IT/Finance to establish a row-level warranty claim ledger — job ID, date, component, failure description, contract type, material cost, labor cost, resolution time. This is a one-time setup that compounds for every future CI cycle.

    Lead Quality + IT
    Compounding Value

  6. Run a Monthly Labor-vs-Parts Ratio as a Leading Indicator

    The 2025→2026 shift from 78% labor to 46% labor is either a real shift or a timing artifact (TT-2P active claims haven't converted to site visits yet). Publish a monthly labor/total ratio per product line. When the ratio moves, the Quality team gets an early signal about whether CI programs are actually reducing truck-roll-driven spend — or whether the 2025 pattern is returning.

    Lead Quality Analytics
    Leading Indicator
About This Report
A structured CI feedback view synthesizing the Warranty Intelligence Dashboard (portfolio coverage, as of Apr 16, 2026) and the Warranty Claims Expenditure Dashboard (claims spend, as of Apr 8, 2026). Built for the Terrasmart Quality team to drive Continuous Improvement prioritization across Design, Process, Supplier, and Training workstreams.
Data Caveats
Claims data is pre-aggregated by product line. Row-level claim detail is not available in the current data layer — this limits monthly trending, per-customer concentration, and MWdc-normalized failure rates. 2026 YTD is a partial year — year-over-year comparisons are directional only.
